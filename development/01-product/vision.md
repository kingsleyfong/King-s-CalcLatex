# Product Vision

## One-Line Summary
King's CalcLatex turns Obsidian into the engineering calculator you wish existed — LaTeX in, answers + graphs out, all inline.

## Goals
1. **Inline-first UX** — Results and graphs appear directly in the editor at note-taking speed
2. **Full CAS** — Symbolic math as powerful as TI-Nspire or Casio fx-CG50
3. **Desmos-quality graphs** — Smooth 2D curves and 3D surfaces with interactive controls
4. **Engineering-ready** — Unit conversions, vector calculus, linear algebra, PDEs
5. **Dual display** — Inline preview for quick results + Graph Inspector for deep work
6. **LLM-developable** — Project structure enables multi-session agentic development

## Target User
2nd-year Mechanical Engineering undergrad at the University of Waterloo using Obsidian for coursework (MECH 201 Advanced Calculus and beyond).

## Non-Goals
- Not a replacement for MATLAB/Mathematica for heavy numerical simulation
- Not a collaborative real-time editor like Google Docs
- Not a standalone app — lives inside Obsidian
