# Acknowledgements & Open-Source Inspirations

**King's CalcLatex** is built upon and inspired by several outstanding open-source projects in the Obsidian and mathematical computing ecosystems. We express our deep gratitude to the original creators and maintainers:

---

## 1. LaTeX Suite (Gilles Castel & Arturo)
- **License**: MIT License
- **Inspiration & Concept**: High-speed LaTeX snippet auto-expansion engine, math mode detection, matrix shortcuts (`pmat`, `bmat`, `vmat`), auto-fractions, and tabstop traversal (`$0`, `$1`, `$2`).
- **Consolidation**: Native CodeMirror 6 ViewPlugin extension and Excalidraw text overlay interceptor ingested in King's CalcLatex v2.3.0.

---

## 2. CalcTex (Developer-Mike)
- **License**: MIT License
- **Inspiration & Concept**: Inline LaTeX expression evaluation and 2D/3D math rendering inside Obsidian notes.

---

## 3. Excalidraw & ExcalidrawAutomate (Zsviczian)
- **License**: MIT License
- **Inspiration & Concept**: Obsidian Excalidraw plugin, ExcalidrawAutomate (`ea`) script engine, and canvas element injection.

---

## 4. Underlying Computation & Graphic Libraries
- **Compute Engine & CortexJS**: Math expression parsing & CAS evaluation.
- **Giac WASM**: Symbolic computer algebra system compiled to WebAssembly.
- **Three.js & Canvas 2D**: High-performance WebGL 3D rendering and 2D interactive graphs.
- **MathJax**: High-fidelity LaTeX SVG math rendering.
